Korean Baseball Sensation - Hyun-Jin Ryu

2006

Ryu debuts as a professional player. He is first picked in the draft by the Hanwha Eagles. Awarded with the Rookie of the Year and Player of the Year (the only ever player to do so in the Korean league). He also wins the Triple Crown for this year.

2008

He represents South Korea in the 2008 Summer Olypmics in baseball. South Korea defeats Cuba in the final match to win the gold medal.

2010

He once again represented South Korea for baseball in the 2010 Asian Games. South Korea wins the gold medal again by defeating Chinese Taipei in the final game.

2013

Ryu debuts in the MLB with the Los Angeles Dodgers. He played his first major league game on April 2nd and won his first major league game on April 7th. On May 28th, he records his first shutout game against the Angels.

2015

After reporting back and shoulder pain, he was put on the injured list by the Dodgers. It was found that his left shoulder labrum was damaged and Ryu underwent surgery. This stopped him from playing for the 2015 season.

2018

Ryu becomes the first Korean pitcher to start a World Series game.

2019

Ryu pitched his second shutout game against the Atlanta Braves on May 7th for a 9-0 victory. He was awarded with Pitcher of the Month for May. He was selected to be the starting pitcher for the National League at the 2019 All-Star Game making it his first appearance at the all-stars. At the end of the season, Ryu came in second for the National Cy Young award.
